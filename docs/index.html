None of these products need a big team. They need two people. One who builds, one who sells. The developer runs the factory — shapes, builds, ships, iterates. The sales person knows the local market — walks into businesses, demos the product, signs customers, feeds back what they hear. Here's what that split looks like.

Solo Dev + Factory

  • ●Build MVPs in weeks, not months
  • ●7 AI agents handle the grunt work
  • ●Ship and iterate on real feedback
  • ●Scales to 3-5 products simultaneously

Sales & Operations

  • ●Find customers in days, not months
  • ●Local market knowledge and connections
  • ●Direct feedback loop to the factory
  • ●Sell across multiple verticals at once

One developer with this factory can build 3-5 products simultaneously. One sales person can sell across all of them. The question isn't whether the tech works — it's which problem is worth solving first.
